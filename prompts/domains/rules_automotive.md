# Domain Rules: Automotive / Vehicle Maintenance
# Source: Atlas Training Hub + Discord + Automotive Service Manual Conventions
# Priority: 3 (below Discord Golden Rules and Technical Glossary)

## Verb Precision
- loosen: turning fastener counterclockwise with tool (wrench, socket, ratchet)
- tighten: turning fastener clockwise with tool
- remove: physically taking away a component after loosening/disconnecting
- install: placing a component into its position (reverse of remove)
- disconnect: separating electrical or fluid connections
- connect: joining electrical or fluid connections
- lift: raising vehicle or heavy component (jack, hoist)
- lower: controlled descent of vehicle or component
- drain: removing fluid (oil, coolant) from system
- fill: adding fluid to system
- bleed: removing air from fluid system (brakes, coolant)
- torque: tightening to specific specification with torque wrench

## Object Naming (Automotive)
- "bolt" for threaded fasteners; "nut" for threaded receivers
- "socket" for socket wrench attachment (not "bit")
- "wrench" for any open/closed end wrench
- "ratchet" for the handle tool (socket goes on ratchet)
- "jack" for any lifting device
- "filter" generic; "oil filter", "air filter" if clearly identifiable
- "hose" for any flexible tube connection
- "bracket" for any mounting hardware
- If unsure of component: use "part" or "component" (Hypernym rule)

## Diagnostic Terms (Observe, Don't Label)
- "hesitation" — engine behavior term. Do NOT label as action.
- "play" — mechanical looseness. Do NOT label as action.
- These are diagnostic observations, NOT hand-object interactions.

## Common Edge Cases (Automotive)
- Crawling under vehicle: do NOT label (navigation, not interaction)
- Using flashlight to look: do NOT label (examining, not hand-object action)
- Wiping hands on rag: do NOT label (not task-relevant unless cleaning a part)
- Supporting a part while loosening: label the loosen action, add "hold [part]" if relevant
